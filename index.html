I am an Associate Professor in the Computer Science Department at UCLA, where I am directing the Vision and Autonomy Intelligence Lab (VAIL); I am also the Chief AI Scientist at Coco Robotics.

My research is at the intersection of computer vision and robot learning, with a recent focus on developing efficient and generalizable physical AI that aligns with humans in complex urban environments. I am also interested in understanding various human-centric properties of current AI models beyond their accuracy, such as explainability, interpretability, steerability, generalization, and safety. Some of the earlier well-known works I co-authored with my great PhD advisor Antonio Torralba are Class Activation Mapping (CAM), Places, ADE20K, Network Dissection.
